Internet Addiction Defined:


Many addiction experts have different definitions of internet addiction. This is primarily because of how internet/gaming disorder, as of 2018, has been recently recognized by the WHO (World Health Organization) as a legitimate disorder. Although, it is important to keep in mind how addiction is a symptom of a larger problem, which commonly includes depression and loneliness. For this website's purposes, we will be defining addiction as such:

The neglect of past customs and the replacement of an integral aspect of their life (connection, feeling of mastery) for priority of internet use.

Forms:


A common misconception of internet addiction is that it is the same thing as gaming addiction. Although gaming addiction is a form of internet addiction, there are many alternate forms of internet addiction. Possible forms include:

  • Social Media Addiction
  • Gaming Addiction
  • Chatroom Addiction
  • Cybersex Addiction
  • Net Compulsion Addiction (Gambling, Shopping, etc.)
  • Information Seeking Addiction
  • Virtual Reality Addiction

Symptoms:

As previously stated, many different experts have different symptoms of internet addiction. Although, symptoms of internet addiction commonly include:

  • Increased amount of time using the internet
  • Neglecting relationships
  • Grade decline
  • Job performance decline
  • Craving more time for internet use
  • Failiure to control behavior
